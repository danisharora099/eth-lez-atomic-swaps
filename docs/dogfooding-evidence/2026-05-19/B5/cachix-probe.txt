Per-derivation Cachix coverage probe for the upstream
`github:logos-co/logos-delivery-module/v0.1.1` build closure on
aarch64-darwin. Captured 2026-05-19.

Method (per-derivation): for each store-path output computed by
`nix derivation show`, query the substituter's narinfo endpoint with a
plain HTTP HEAD. A `200` means the substituter (cachix.org or
cache.nixos.org) has the NAR available and a fresh consumer would
fetch instead of build; a `404` means the consumer's `nix build` would
fall through to building locally.

This is exactly the lookup `nix build` performs at evaluation time —
it does not depend on what is currently in the local Nix store, so it
answers "what would a fresh aarch64-darwin downstream see today?".

Interpretation:

- The four heavy derivations named in the dogfooding finding —
  `zerokit-0.9.0-vendor-staging`, `zerokit-0.9.0`,
  `liblogosdelivery-dev`, `logos-delivery_module-module-lib-1.1.0` —
  are 404 on **both** substituters. So is the published artefact
  itself (`logos-delivery_module-module-lib-lgx-1.1.0`, both the
  `#lgx-portable` and the `#lgx` / `-dev` flavours), as is the
  `logos-delivery_module-module-lib-bundle-1.1.0` intermediate.
- The `logos-cpp-sdk-*` derivations and `logos-module-0.1.0` *are*
  served by `logos-co.cachix.org` (200). So the Logos Cachix push
  pipeline runs and works — it is just not pushing the
  `logos-delivery-module` half of the build graph.
- `zerokit-0.9.0-vendor{-staging,}` are fixed-output derivations (FOD;
  `outputHashMode = "recursive"`, `outputHash` declared); 404 on
  Cachix is less impactful than for non-FOD derivations because Nix
  will re-fetch from upstream sources rather than rebuild. The
  compile-time cost of the finding is therefore borne by
  `zerokit-0.9.0`, `liblogosdelivery-dev`, and the
  `logos-delivery_module-module-lib` chain.
- No probe was performed for `x86_64-linux` /
  `linux-amd64` / `x86_64-darwin` — the dogfooding session ran on
  `aarch64-darwin` and the same gap there is what justifies the
  finding. The Cachix narinfo probe is identical in shape for any
  system; the upstream maintainer can rerun it themselves.
