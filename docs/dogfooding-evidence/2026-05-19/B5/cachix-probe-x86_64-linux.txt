Interpretation:

- The pattern is identical to the aarch64-darwin baseline: every
  derivation called out by the dogfooding finding
  (`zerokit-*`, `liblogosdelivery-dev`,
  `logos-delivery_module-module-lib{,-bundle,-lgx}-1.1.0` in both
  flavours) is 404 on both substituters, while `logos-cpp-sdk-*` and
  `logos-module-0.1.0` are 200 on `logos-co.cachix.org`. So a fresh
  x86_64-linux downstream consumer faces the same cold-build cost as
  aarch64-darwin.
- `zerokit-0.9.0-vendor-staging` resolves to the same FOD output hash
  (`lv5fw3xbiay58dasr6lcmhyxpmrbvi6n-zerokit-0.9.0-vendor-staging`)
  across systems because it is `outputHashMode = "recursive"` with
  declared `outputHash`. The other zerokit-vendor / zerokit /
  liblogosdelivery / module-lib / lgx paths are system-specific (the
  unredacted hashes in `README.md` differ from the aarch64-darwin row).
