Interpretation:

- The gap is **strictly wider on x86_64-darwin** than on aarch64-darwin
  or x86_64-linux: every probed derivation — including the
  `logos-cpp-sdk-*` trio and `logos-module-0.1.0` that act as the
  "cache push works, just not for delivery-module half" control on
  the other two systems — is 404 on both substituters. So a fresh
  x86_64-darwin consumer of the upstream flake would build the
  *entire* `logos-cpp-sdk-* → logos-module → liblogosdelivery →
  zerokit → logos-delivery_module-module-lib*` chain locally, not
  just the delivery-module half.
- This is consistent with the simpler hypothesis that the Logos
  Cachix push pipeline does not currently target the x86_64-darwin
  system at all (rather than targeting it but skipping the
  delivery-module subgraph), but the probe itself only proves "no
  artefacts present"; the upstream maintainer can confirm whether
  x86_64-darwin is in the supported-systems matrix and whether the
  cache pipeline runs for it.
- `zerokit-0.9.0-vendor-staging` resolves to the same FOD output
  hash (`lv5fw3xbiay58dasr6lcmhyxpmrbvi6n-zerokit-0.9.0-vendor-staging`)
  as on aarch64-darwin and x86_64-linux because the FOD's recursive
  output hash is system-independent. The non-FOD paths in the table
  are x86_64-darwin-specific (see `README.md` for the unredacted hashes).
